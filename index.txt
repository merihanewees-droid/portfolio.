        <div class="sample-tags"><span class="stag">SEO product guide</span><span class="stag">Scar care brand</span></div>
      </div>
      <h3>How to Choose the Right Silicone Scar Sheets After Surgery</h3>
      <p class="excerpt">Written for a scar-care brand's silicone sheet line, targeting post-surgical patients searching for scar treatment. The piece opens on the reader's real question — <em>why do some scars heal smoothly and others don't</em> — before explaining the clinical mechanism (transepidermal water loss and collagen regulation) in plain terms, then maps each product variant to a specific surgery type, scar size, and body area.</p>
      <div class="breakdown">
        <div class="b-item"><div class="b-label">Written for</div><p>A direct-to-consumer scar-care brand selling through pharmacy and online retail across the Gulf region.</p></div>
<div class="b-item"><div class="b-label">Voice &amp; tone</div><p>Reassuring but not soft — leads with mechanism and evidence, avoids hype language, treats the reader as capable of understanding "why," not just "what to buy."</p></div>
        <div class="b-item"><div class="b-label">Clinically</div><p>Correctly explains occlusive barrier therapy and TEWL, cites peer-reviewed sourcing, and flags contraindications for open wounds — the kind of safety note pharma legal teams check for first.</p></div>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="sample">
      <div class="sample-top">
        <div class="s-index">02</div>
        <div class="sample-tags"><span class="stag">Condition explainer</span><span class="stag">Scar care brand</span></div>
      </div>
      <h3>A Tailored Solution for Different Types of Surgical Scars</h3>
      <p class="excerpt">A companion piece to the guide above, going deeper on the clinical differences between flat, hypertrophic, and keloid scars. Rather than leading with the product, it educates first — helping the reader identify what type of scar they actually have — then routes them to the right product and, just as importantly, to a clear set of signs that mean it's time to see a dermatologist.</p>
      <div class="breakdown">
        <div class="b-item"><div class="b-label">Written for</div><p>The same scar-care brand, positioned as an educational follow-up article to build search authority around scar types.</p></div>
        <div class="b-item"><div class="b-label">Voice &amp; tone</div><p>Slightly more clinical than the product guide — this piece is trust-building content, so it prioritizes being genuinely useful over converting a sale on the first read.</p></div>
        <div class="b-item"><div class="b-label">Clinically</div><p>Distinguishes scar pathology by real risk factors — skin tone, family history, wound tension — and includes an appropriate escalation trigger for professional care.</p></div>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="sample">
      <div class="sample-top">
        <div class="s-index">03</div>
        <div class="sample-tags"><span class="stag">Facility &amp; service copy</span><span class="stag">Hospital group</span></div>
      </div>
      <h3>How an Airport Medical Center Provides 24/7 Immediate Care</h3>
      <p class="excerpt">Written for a hospital group's airport-based medical center, aimed at travelers and airport staff. The piece frames the service around a specific, high-anxiety moment — symptoms appearing mid-journey, no time to search for care — walks through each department's offering, and closes with a clear medical-center-versus-hospital decision framework so readers know when each is the right call.</p>
      <div class="breakdown">
        <div class="b-item"><div class="b-label">Written for</div><p>A multi-branch hospital group's flagship airport clinic, part of a broader content series linking branch pages together.</p></div>

        <div class="b-item"><div class="b-label">Clinically</div><p>Lists jet lag symptoms accurately against clinical sourcing and draws an appropriate triage line between center-level and emergency-level care.</p></div>

      <h2>Same process, every brief</h2>
      <p class="sec-desc">Whether it's a single article or an ongoing content account, this is the pipeline behind each piece.</p>

      <div class="p-step">
