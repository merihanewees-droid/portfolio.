Scar Care Guide

How to Select the Right Silicone Sheets for Your Surgery Scar

Have you ever wondered why some scars heal smoothly while others remain visible for years? Medical-grade silicone scar sheets are the gold standard in modern scar care — the real difference is choosing the right sheet for your scar's shape, size, and location.

How Do Silicone Sheets Help Improve Scar Healing?

Newly formed skin loses moisture abnormally fast, a process called transepidermal water loss. This dehydration signals the skin to overproduce collagen — the cause of raised, visible scars. Silicone sheets restore the skin's moisture balance and interrupt that signal.

Which Sheet Matches Your Surgery Type?
  • C-Section — hydrates and softens post-partum incision lines.
  • Tummy Tuck — a longer format sized for abdominal incisions.
  • Facial / Acne — lightweight, nearly invisible for daily wear.

Scar Care Guide

A Tailored Solution for Different Types of Surgical Scars

After surgery, it's natural to wonder how your scar will heal. Understanding the type of scar you have is an important first step — and with early, consistent care, it's possible to support smoother healing over time.

What Are the Different Types of Scars After Surgery?
  • Flat (mature) scars — settle and lighten naturally over time.
  • Hypertrophic scars — raised, firm, stay within the wound area.
  • Keloid scars — raised scars that grow beyond the original wound.
How Do Silicone Sheets Support Surgical Scars?

By acting as an occlusive barrier, silicone sheets reduce transepidermal water loss and help regulate excess collagen formation, one of the main causes of raised scars, so scars can become smoother and less visible over time.

When to See a Doctor

If a new scar quickly becomes very raised, itchy, or extends beyond the original wound, or you have a family history of keloids, it's worth seeing a dermatologist early for tailored treatment.
